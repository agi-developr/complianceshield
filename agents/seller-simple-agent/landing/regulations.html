FTC Compliance
Federal Trade Commission rules governing advertising, endorsements, and consumer protection
Endorsement Guides
16 CFR Part 255
FTC
Requires clear disclosure of material connections between endorsers and advertisers. Covers sponsored content, affiliate links, gifted products, and paid partnerships.
View Official Source
Unfair or Deceptive Acts
FTC Act Section 5
FTC
Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts or practices in commerce. The foundational statute that underpins all FTC enforcement against misleading advertising and fraudulent business practices.
View Official Source
Made in USA Standards
16 CFR Part 323
FTC
Regulates "Made in USA" and similar origin claims. Products must be "all or virtually all" made in the United States to use unqualified domestic origin claims.
View Official Source
Environmental Marketing Claims
16 CFR Part 260 (Green Guides)
FTC
Guides marketers on making truthful environmental claims. Covers terms like "recyclable," "biodegradable," "compostable," and "carbon neutral" to prevent greenwashing.
View Official Source

Health / FDA
Food and Drug Administration regulations on labeling, health claims, and product safety
Food Labeling
21 CFR Part 101
FDA
Governs nutrition labeling, nutrient content claims, and health claims for food products. Requires specific formatting and substantiation for any health-related statements.
View Official Source
Food, Drug & Cosmetic Act
FDCA (21 U.S.C. Ch. 9)
FDA
The foundational statute giving FDA authority over food, drugs, medical devices, and cosmetics. Prohibits misbranding and adulteration, with significant penalties for violations.
View Official Source
Dietary Supplement Labeling
DSHEA (21 U.S.C. 343)
FDA
Regulates labeling and marketing of dietary supplements. Requires "structure/function" claim disclaimers and prohibits unapproved disease treatment claims.
View Official Source
Drug Advertising
21 CFR Part 202
FDA
Governs prescription drug advertising. Requires fair balance between benefit claims and risk disclosures, and prohibits false or misleading drug promotion.
View Official Source

Financial / SEC
Securities and Exchange Commission rules and FINRA standards governing financial communications
Anti-Fraud Rule
SEC Rule 10b-5
SEC
Prohibits fraud, material misstatements, and omissions in connection with securities transactions. Applies to any content promoting stocks, crypto, or investment opportunities.
View Official Source
Communications with the Public
FINRA Rule 2210
FINRA
Governs how broker-dealers communicate with the public. Requires fair, balanced, and non-misleading communications about investment products and financial services.
View Official Source
Investment Advisers Act
15 U.S.C. 80b-1 et seq.
SEC
Regulates investment advisers and prohibits fraudulent advisory practices. Relevant for creators offering financial advice, stock picks, or investment recommendations.
View Official Source
Crypto Asset Guidance
SEC Framework for Digital Assets
SEC
SEC guidance on whether crypto tokens qualify as securities. Critical for creators promoting cryptocurrencies, NFTs, or token launches in their content.
View Official Source

Privacy
Data protection and privacy laws governing how content handles user data and audience targeting
Children's Online Privacy
COPPA (15 U.S.C. Ch. 91)
COPPA
Protects privacy of children under 13 online. Requires verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information. Critical for family-friendly content creators.
View Official Source
California Consumer Privacy Act
CCPA (Cal. Civ. Code 1798.100)
CCPA
Gives California consumers rights over their personal data including the right to know, delete, and opt-out of sale. Applies to businesses meeting revenue or data thresholds.
View Official Source
General Data Protection Regulation
GDPR (EU 2016/679)
GDPR
EU regulation requiring lawful basis for data processing, user consent, and data protection by design. Applies to any creator with EU-based viewers or customers.
View Official Source
CAN-SPAM Act
15 U.S.C. 7701 et seq.
CAN-SPAM
Regulates commercial email and electronic messaging. Requires opt-out mechanisms, truthful headers, and clear identification of advertising content in emails.
View Official Source

Advertising Standards
Industry self-regulatory standards and truth-in-advertising requirements
Lanham Act - False Advertising
15 U.S.C. 1125(a)
Advertising
Federal statute prohibiting false advertising and misleading representations in commercial advertising. Enables competitors to sue for damages from deceptive ad claims.
View Official Source
NAD Advertising Guidelines
National Advertising Division
NAD
Self-regulatory body that reviews national advertising for truthfulness. Evaluates performance claims, testimonials, and comparative advertising across all media.
View Official Source
Testimonials & Reviews
FTC Testimonial Guidelines
FTC
Requires that testimonials reflect typical consumer experiences. Prohibits fake reviews, undisclosed paid reviews, and misleading "results not typical" disclaimers.
View Official Source
Alcohol Advertising Standards
TTB / FTC Joint Guidance
TTB
Regulates advertising of alcoholic beverages including age-gating requirements, prohibited health claims, and restrictions on targeting minors in promotional content.
View Official Source

Consumer Protection
State and federal laws protecting consumers from deceptive practices and unfair business conduct
Truth in Lending Act
TILA (15 U.S.C. 1601)
Consumer
Requires clear disclosure of credit terms and costs. Relevant for creators promoting financial products, BNPL services, or credit-based offers to audiences.
View Official Source
Consumer Product Safety
CPSA (15 U.S.C. 2051)
CPSC
Governs safety of consumer products. Content promoting recalled products or making safety claims must comply. Especially relevant for product review creators.
View Official Source
Warranty Disclosure Rules
Magnuson-Moss (15 U.S.C. 2301)
Consumer
Regulates warranties on consumer products. Requires clear disclosure of warranty terms and prohibits deceptive warranty-related claims in product promotions.
View Official Source
